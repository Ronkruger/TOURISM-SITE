About Tanjay City

Tanjay city is a small city in the province of Negros Oriental, known for its agricultural products such as rice, corn, and vegetables. The city also has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, Spanish, and American influences. The local dialect spoken in Tanjay City is Cebuano, but many residents also speak Tagalog and English.

Delicacies

Budbud kabog - a local delicacy made from ground millet wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

Binagol - a sweet snack made from smashed taro, coconut milk, and sugar, wrapped in banana leaves and baked.

Maja blanca - a creamy coconut milk, cornstarch, and sugar.

Tableya - a chocolate drink made from locally-grown cacao beans, often served hot with milk and sugar.
